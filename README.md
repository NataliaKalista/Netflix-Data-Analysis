# Netflix Content Strategy Analysis

## Project Overview
This project explores the Netflix dataset to understand the platform's content strategy. I analyzed the evolution of content over time, the balance between Movies and TV Shows, and the geographical distribution of productions.

**Key Question:** How has Netflix's library evolved, and what are the dominant characteristics of its content?

## Tools & Technologies
* **Python** (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn)
* **Techniques:** Data Cleaning (handling missing values), String Manipulation, Time Series Analysis.

## Key Insights

### 1. Content Type Distribution
Netflix is predominantly a movie platform, with **Movies** accounting for nearly 70% of the library compared to TV Shows.

![Content Type](Plots/)

### 2. Exponential Growth
The platform saw massive exponential growth starting around **2015/2016**, coinciding with its global expansion strategy.

![Growth](Plots/netflix_content_pace.png)

### 3. Top Producing Countries
While the **USA** dominates, **India** holds a strong second place, highlighting the importance of Bollywood. The UK and Japan (Anime) follow.

![Countries](netflix_countries.png)

### 4. Movie Duration Analysis
The distribution of movie runtimes follows a normal distribution with a peak around **90-100 minutes**. The average movie duration is approx. **99 minutes**, aligning with industry standards.

![Duration](netflix_duration.png)

## Conclusion
Netflix has successfully transitioned from a US-centric service to a global content hub, with a strong focus on feature-length movies and increasing investment in international markets (especially India).
